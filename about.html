About Chrono Trigger

Background

Hironobu Sakaguchi (left), Yuji Horii (center), and Akira Toriyama (right).

The second most listened Chrono Trigger song on Spotify: Secret of the Forest, composed by Yasunori Mitsuda.

Chrono Trigger is a video game developed by Squaresoft and released on the Super Nintendo Entertainment System in 1995. The designers for the project were known as the Dream Team, consisting of: the creator of the game Final Fantasy, Hironobu Sakaguchi; the creator of the game Dragon Quest, Yuji Horii; and the creator of the famous manga Dragon Ball, Akira Toriyama.
Chrono Trigger would be ported and remade on various consoles, including the PlayStation, the Nintendo DS, Android, iOS, and PC. I recommend playing the SNES, DS, or PC versions.

Plot

Crono, Frog, and Lucca riding the Epoch, a time machine that can fly.

The theme that plays during one of the last areas of the game: Black Omen, composed by Yasunori Mitsuda.

Chrono Trigger starts in the year 1000 AD on Earth, where a fair is taking place for the one thousandth anniversary of the kingdom. Crono's friend Lucca and her dad invented a teleportation device to show off at the Millenial Fair but instead of teleporting, they time travel instead through Time Gates. They travel to various eras, however travelling to the post-apocalyptic future of 2300 AD would allow them to see events that happened in 1999 AD that destroyed the Earth, caused by an entity called Lavos. Crono and his friends' goal is now to defeat Lavos in the past and change the future.

Soundtrack

Yasunori Mitsuda (left) and Nobuo Uematsu (right).

The main theme for the game: Chrono Trigger, composed by Yasunori Mitsuda.

The soundtrack for Chrono Trigger was composed mainly by Yasunori Mitsuda. Mitsuda was disappointed by his salary and his current position as a sound designer, and demanded that Squaresoft either make him a composer for a project, or he would quit. He would then overwork himself to the point where he developed stomach ulcers and had to hand off the rest of the music to Final Fantasy's composer, Nobuo Uematsu.

Characters

The 600 AD theme: Wind Scene, composed by Yasunori Mitsuda.

Six of the seven playable characters in Chrono Trigger, featured on the Japanese box art. From left to right: Frog, Ayla, Lucca, Robo, Marle, Crono.

Chrono Trigger has a cast made up of people from different time periods. The characters were designed by the creator of Dragon Ball, Akira Toriyama, and his style is reflected in Chrono Trigger. Click the tabs below to find out more about each character.

Crono

The main character in Chrono Trigger. He is the first playable character from 1000 AD, and wields Light magic.

Marle

The first character you meet at the Millenial Fair. She is the princess of the kingdom whom the King dislikes for being too tomboyish. She uses Water magic in the form of Ice.

Lucca

Crono's friend who invents things with her father. She makes the teleportation machine that sets off the events of the game. Lucca uses fire magic.

Frog

After first travelling to the year 600 AD, they meet a knight named Frog, who is a human turned into a frog. Frog uses Water magic.

Robo

Robo is a robot from the year 2300 AD. As he is not a human, he cannot use magic, but his lasers are equivalent to Shadow magic.

Ayla

Ayla is a prehistoric human from 65,000,000 BC. She cannot use magic as magic hadn't been invented in her time, but her attack strength makes up for it.

Gameplay

The North American box art for Chrono Trigger, featuring Crono, Marle, and Frog performing the Arc Impulse (Frost Arc) Triple Technique.

The theme that plays during battle: Battle 1, composed by Yasunori Mitsuda.

Gameplay is similar to that of a standard role-playing game. You control a party of three around a world map and enter various locations. When you get into a battle, you can use Attacks, Techniques, or use an Item. Each character has a Hit Point meter, a Magic Point meter, and a stamina meter. You must wait for the stamina meter to fill before the character can do something, and once they do an action their stamina meter goes back to zero. Characters can increase their Attack and Defense stats by switching equipment or using items.

You can also use Techniques, which take MP to use, but allows a character to deal more damage, hit multiple enemies, or apply a status effect. Characters can also combine Techniques to make Dual and Triple Techs.
